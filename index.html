Architecture

The core invariant: all revenues route to the Vault and are managed via deterministic zone rules. No admin drains. No governance rerouting of funds.

Zone 1 — LP Regulation

The Vault maintains DEX health by keeping LP within a target band (10–20% of market cap, target ~15%). Regulation is event-driven and bounded.

  • Core LP is Vault-owned and permanently locked
  • Protection against thin liquidity + extreme slippage
  • Bounded actions (no runaway buy/sell loops)

Zone 2 — LoC Redemption Rails

Dedicated liquidity rails for LoC redemptions: Redeem Buffer + Short-term pool. This zone is reserved for redemption obligations and cannot be repurposed.

  • Queue + throttle during stress (fail-soft)
  • No forced liquidation of long-term holdings
  • Predictable maturity buckets

Zone 3 — Long-term Vault

Surplus only (after LP + Zone 2 funding) may be allocated to conservative long-term strategies. This zone is never used for redemptions.

  • Conservative strategy allowlist
  • No leverage policy (baseline)
  • Fails closed if strategies are unavailable

LoC — Letters of Credit

LoC is a monthly batch instrument issued only from realized Vault profits. It is not a stablecoin and has no peg defense.

Issuance

  • Monthly batch issuance from realized profits only
  • Stakers receive at 100% face value
  • Non-stakers can preorder at 110% (premium routes to Vault)

Redemption

  • Redeem early at 90% (fail-soft + protects long-term zone)
  • Redeem at 100% after 12 months
  • Secondary market trades pay 1% fee in AF to Vault
Invariant: LoC issuance ≤ realized profits. If profits are low, issuance is low (or pauses).

FAQ

Is this financial advice?

No. This is informational software documentation. Nothing here is a recommendation.

Is AF a stablecoin?

No. There is no peg and no peg defense mechanism. LoC is a time-based credit instrument issued from realized profits only.

Can governance redirect funds?

No. Core economic routing is immutable: 100% of revenues route to the Vault. Governance cannot change economic flows.

What happens if adoption is low?

Fail-soft: issuance slows or pauses. The system is designed to survive low activity without forced insolvency behaviors.
